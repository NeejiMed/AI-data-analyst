# Analysis Playbooks & Investigation Guides

## Revenue Decline Investigation
When revenue declines quarter-over-quarter:
1. Check if decline is seasonal (Q2 is always weaker — expected)
2. Segment by region to identify geographic concentration
3. Segment by product category to identify product-specific issues
4. Check refund rate — spike in refunds can mask true revenue decline
5. Review customer segment mix — enterprise churn has outsized impact
6. Compare to same quarter prior year for seasonality-adjusted view

## Anomaly Investigation Checklist
For any flagged anomaly:
- Spike in Q4: Likely seasonal, verify against prior year Q4
- Dip in Q2: Likely seasonal, only investigate if > 35% below average
- Mid-year spike: Investigate marketing campaigns, product launches
- Mid-year dip: Investigate customer complaints, competitor activity

## Customer Segmentation Action Guide
- Champions (score 10–12): Reward loyalty, offer early access to new features
- Loyal (score 8–9): Upsell to higher tier, offer annual contracts
- Promising (score 6–7): Nurture with targeted campaigns, onboarding check-in
- At Risk (score 4–5): Immediate outreach, understand pain points, offer discount
- Lost (score 1–3): Win-back campaign, survey to understand churn reasons

## Refund Rate Analysis
Current refund rate: ~20% — above the 15% target.
Investigation steps:
1. Segment refunds by product category
2. Identify if specific regions have higher refund rates
3. Check if refund rate correlates with discount percentage
4. Review time-to-refund — immediate refunds suggest product mismatch
